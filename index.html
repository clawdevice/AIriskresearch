Teens Trust AI More Than They Should

A study of Canadian high school students’ AI risk perception (GTA, 2025)

At a Glance

  • Study period: June–September 2025
  • Survey responses: 122
  • Interviews: 32 in-person interviews
  • Schools: 16 GTA schools

How students use AI

  • ~55% are frequent school AI users (more than once/week)
  • Senior grades use AI more than juniors
  • Female students reported slightly higher frequent use than male students

Main risk pattern

  • Overall risk awareness is relatively low
  • Highest concern: learning / critical-thinking impact
  • Lowest concern: privacy & cybersecurity

Frequent users

  • Frequent users show lower concern across all risk categories
  • Normalization of AI may reduce perceived danger

Emotional use

  • A minority use AI for emotional support
  • Some view AI as non-judgmental and empathetic
  • Others maintain clear boundaries (“AI is not my therapist”)

Implications

For Schools

Integrate AI literacy, misinformation detection, privacy basics, and healthy usage boundaries.

For Parents

Discuss AI habits openly, especially privacy exposure and emotional dependence risks.

For Policymakers

Strengthen youth AI safety standards and support nationwide digital literacy initiatives.
